Where Civilization Began

The Yellow River (Huang He) is more than just water; it is the soul of the nation. Flowing through the heart of Zhengzhou, it marks the transition from the mountains to the vast plains.

Its water carries the loess soil, giving it a distinct golden color and making it the most sediment-laden river in the world. It is a landscape of majestic beauty and historical weight.

Scenic Highlights

Must-see spots along the Zhengzhou bank

Emperors Yan & Huang

Two colossal statues carved into the mountain, overlooking the river. They represent the ancestors of the Chinese nation, standing 106 meters tall.

The "Hanging River"

Witness the unique geological phenomenon where the riverbed is actually higher than the city ground level due to centuries of sediment buildup.

The Old Railway Bridge

The "First Bridge of the Yellow River," built in 1903. A historic steel structure that played a vital role in China's industrial development.
